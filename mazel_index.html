About

I am a M.S. candidate at Simmons School of Library and Information Science ('19). I also have a Ph.D. in English Language and Literature from the University of Michigan (2014).

My research explores both the print past and the digital future. As an information scientist, I specialize in cultural heritage informatics and digital stewardship. As a literary historian, I specialize in British poetry and poetics of the long nineteenth century. My goal is to combine these two fields to become a digital humanist.

My work experience is similarly panoramic: I have 15+ years of experience in higher education, cultural institutions, and nonprofit organizations, from the Metropolitan Museum of Art, to New York University and the University of Michigan, to the Center for Reproductive Rights.

I am also a committed runner: I have completed nine marathons, including New York, Chicago, and Boston, with a personal record of 3:02 (2012).

I am married to the remarkable Liz Kaziunas.
